Sofia, Tom,

Confirmed today — Cosmos Hotels signed with [redacted competitor] this afternoon.
We are out. They will run their existing HelixPay integration to end of contract
(May 31) then off-board. ARR loss to us: SGD 120K (the renewal would have been at SGD 165K).

Quick debrief.

**Why we lost.** Multi-property reporting. Cosmos has 47 properties across 3 countries
and they need group-level aggregated reporting — by property, by country, by P&L
center. Our reporting is per-merchant and they need to manually consolidate 47
exports every month. The competitor offers consolidated reporting natively, including
a property-tree concept that maps to their org structure.

This was the issue from the start. I flagged it to Hannah's team in May 2025. It went
on the roadmap and stayed there. In late February when we presented our renewal
proposal Cosmos's CFO told me bluntly that the multi-property thing was disqualifying
unless we could commit to a build with a hard date in 2026. I went to Hannah, she
couldn't commit because of Confluence priorities. We presented a workaround (custom
exports + a quarterly review with Tom) and they politely declined.

**Lessons (in order of importance):**

1. **Multi-property reporting is now the #1 enterprise blocker for SEA hospitality.**
   Sundara Hotels (11 properties, ~SGD 340K open opportunity) is asking the same
   question. Pacific QSR Holdings (chain accounts) is asking similar questions about
   chain rollups. If we lose Sundara on the same gap I expect that's another ~SGD 200K
   ARR.

2. **Workarounds aren't credible at the enterprise tier.** Cosmos was polite but
   the consolidation export workflow we proposed was the wrong answer for a CFO who
   has 47 properties. We need to stop pretending CSM-mediated workarounds are an
   enterprise answer.

3. **Pricing wasn't the issue.** They signed with competitor at a similar price point.
   This was purely product.

**What I'd ask of product:** a real go/no-go decision on multi-property reporting
for 2026, communicated to sales. Either it's funded with a delivery date we can
sell against, or it's deprioritized and we know we're out of the chain hospitality
segment for the year.
