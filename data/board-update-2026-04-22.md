Two-week cadence update ahead of the May 12 meeting. Three things to flag, in order of importance.

**1. Confluence is slipping to Q3.**

We are re-baselining the engineering platform unification program ("Confluence") from end-of-Q2 to end-of-Q3. The cause is the merchant ID schema migration which is the foundation for everything downstream. Dual-write reconciliation is at 99.6% match, we need 100% before cutover, and the long tail of edge cases is taking longer than estimated. Daniel will have a re-baselined plan with milestones by April 30 and we'll walk it through at the May meeting.

This affects the timing of the Codos engagement value as well. We had assumed agent infrastructure could light up in Q3 on top of the unified platform. That now slides to Q4. Codos kicked off this week, scope is unchanged.

**2. Q2 revenue is at risk.**

Q1 closed at SGD 14.2M against 16M plan (–11%). Q2 pacing is currently 26% behind plan three weeks in. The gap is concentrated in Brasil SMB; SEA is roughly on plan. Sofia is reviewing the Q2 number; we'll come to the May meeting with either a revised target or a credible plan to hit the original. Boon will have the analysis by April 30.

We are not in cash trouble — runway is 18 months. We are in growth-rate trouble.

Aggregate NPS is 47 (SEA enterprise 62, SEA SMB 41, Brasil enterprise 53, Brasil SMB 31). The Brasil SMB number dropped 8 points QoQ; CS attributes it to the Q1 case-volume spike that coincided with two CSM departures. We are hiring back. Three enterprise churns in Q1 representing SGD 340K ARR; one was to a competitor (Cosmos Hotels), one was pricing (Lazada SG midmarket), one was a bank-internal decision (Banco Verde).

I want to flag for transparency: at the all-hands last week we communicated the SEA-enterprise NPS number (62) as the headline. The aggregate (47) is the more honest framing. Marco and Sofia are aligning on consistent external messaging.
