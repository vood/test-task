## Phase 2: Knowledge & Expertise

### Q: How was Q1 for Brasil?

**A:** olha, was not good. we missed by about twenty percent on revenue. we did four point eight million sgd against a target of six. the reasons — para falar a verdade — three things. first deal size compression, the deals we closed were smaller than the deals we forecasted, average deal size dropped meaningfully versus q4. second the segment shift, we are seeing more interest from outside são paulo where the merchants are smaller and the cycles are different and we haven't tuned the playbook for that. third competitive pricing pressure, the local players are aggressive on rate especially for tap and we lost some deals on price that we would have won six months ago. i own the miss. sofia and i talked about it the first week of april.

### Q: What's the pipeline coverage looking like for Q2?

**A:** one point four times. way below where i want to be. sea is at two point six i think. we need to be at two minimum to feel comfortable about the quarter. so april is a build pipeline month for us.

### Q: How is the team performing on activity?

**A:** mixed. four of my twelve are below where they need to be on activity. two of those four are on pips — formal performance plans. the other two we're working with. it's not a fun part of the job but the team needs to see consistency on standards.

### Q: Tell me about the Tap product and how it's landing in Brasil.

**A:** tap launched in brasil end of last year and the form factor is great, merchants love that they don't need a terminal. but the deals are smaller than what we used to close on the core product because tap attracts smaller merchants. and the bigger problem is the tap-loyalty integration. when a merchant runs both products there's a bug in the reconciliation report — the loyalty redemptions and the tap transactions don't line up correctly. the merchants see this. it's hurting our attach rate on loyalty because once a merchant sees one bad report they stop trusting the data. larissa mendes had a deal last month where the prospect literally pulled up our reconciliation report on a call, pointed at a discrepancy, and asked her to explain it. she couldn't.

## Phase 3: Processes & Tools

### Q: How is the team feeling about the migration?

### Q: Anything else on tooling?
