## Phase 2: Knowledge & Expertise

### Q: Talk to me about q1. What closed and what didn't.

**A:** two big ones closed. a regional qsr chain with a hundred and ten locations signed march twenty-eight, that was a four and a half month cycle started in november. and a logistics company march twelve, slightly faster cycle there because they had a hard deadline on their existing vendor. combined those two are about one point four million arr. on the loss side cosmos hotels which i'm sure you've heard about, that one hurt.

### Q: Walk me through cosmos.

**A:** cosmos has forty seven properties across malaysia and thailand and they wanted multi-property reporting. they wanted to see consolidated payment data across all forty seven properties with property-level breakdowns and roll-ups for the parent group. we can't do that today. you can run reports per merchant id but there's no aggregation layer that lets a parent see across child properties. i flagged this gap to product about eleven months ago. i wrote it up after a different deal where the same thing came up. it didn't get prioritized. cosmos went to a competitor six weeks ago specifically for that. losing a deal on price you can stomach. losing a deal on a feature you've been asking for for a year is harder.

### Q: Is this a one-off or a pattern?

**A:** it's a pattern. every single enterprise deal i'm in asks about multi-property reporting. every one. hotels chains qsr chains retail anyone with multiple locations. usually they accept the workaround which is exporting per-merchant and stitching it themselves but the bigger they are the less they tolerate that. cosmos just had the most leverage to walk.

### Q: What part of the enterprise sales motion is the most painful?

**A:** getting product or engineering to come on a call when the prospect needs a real answer. the prospects always have technical questions — how does the api handle x, what's the latency, can you do this kind of integration — and i can answer maybe sixty percent of them. for the other forty i need an actual engineer or a pm. and right now everyone in eng is heads down on confluence and the pms are stretched. i've had calls where i had to say let me get back to you and that response time has cost us momentum. when i first joined an engineer would jump on within twenty four hours, now it's a week or it doesn't happen.
