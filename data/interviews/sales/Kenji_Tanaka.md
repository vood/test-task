## Phase 1: Role & Daily Work

### Q: Kenji thanks for taking the time. What do you do day to day?

**A:** outbound sales for sea smb mostly singapore. i call merchants from a list and try to qualify them and book a meeting or close a small deal directly. been here four months. report into aisha.

### Q: What does a typical day look like?

**A:** dial dial dial. honestly. i'm at the desk by nine and i'm dialing by nine fifteen and i don't really stop until six. lunch is usually fast. the target is eighty calls a day so the day is structured around hitting that number.

## Phase 2: Knowledge & Expertise

### Q: How do you feel about the 80 calls a day target?

**A:** brutal honestly. i hit maybe fifty to sixty on a normal day. and that's me actually trying. about a quarter of the dials don't pick up at all so those are essentially zero contact. of the rest a lot are short because the merchant says not interested and hangs up. so the actual conversation count is way below the dial count. and probably half the leads on my list are recycled from archived leads — people we tried before and parked. you can tell because the notes on the record say things like spoke six months ago not ready. i don't know why we're calling them again with no new context.

### Q: Do you know where the eighty number comes from?

**A:** no. i was told eighty in onboarding. i assumed it came from somewhere like a benchmark or a previous quarter number. nobody told me the origin.

### Q: What's the typical deal size you close?

**A:** under a thousand sgd. very small. these are smb merchants, mostly cafes small retailers, very price sensitive. they compare us to two or three competitors and the conversation is usually about the rate not the product.

## Phase 3: Processes & Tools

### Q: How is hubspot for you?

**A:** mostly fine. i learned it in onboarding and it works for what i do. the dialer integration broke about three weeks ago and only got fixed last week so for that whole window the dialer was working but the calls weren't logging properly into hubspot. i lost a lot of activity tracking in that gap. some of my dials don't show up in my numbers because of it which is frustrating when the manager looks at my activity report.

### Q: Anything else weird with the data?

**A:** yeah some of the older contact records are missing notes. like i open a record and there's a deal history but no call notes attached. people on the team say that happened during the migration from pipedrive. it's not blocking but it's annoying when i'm trying to figure out the history before i call.

### Q: Do you use Gong or any call coaching tool?

**A:** no. i know we have it i think aisha mentioned it once but i've never logged in. i don't think outbound is set up on it, just the aes.

## Phase 5: Problems & Ideas

### Q: If something could help you do your job better what would it be?

**A:** screen the leads before i call them. if ai could go through my list and tell me which fifteen are actually worth calling today and which fifty are zombies that should be archived, i'd hit my real target on the worth calling fifteen and not waste the day on the zombies. right now the screening is me doing it manually in the first thirty seconds of each call which is the most expensive way to do it.
