## Phase 1: Role & Daily Work

### Q: Aisha hi — quick one to start, what do you actually run day to day?

**A:** i run the sea sales floor in singapore which is five reps three aes and two outbound and i report into sofia. so i carry the sea number for the quarter and i'm the person who looks at the pipeline every monday and tells sofia what i think we land. day to day i'm in pipeline reviews coaching calls and forecasting and probably more time than i'd like in hubspot cleaning up records that should not need cleaning.

### Q: Walk me through a typical week.

**A:** monday morning forecast call with sofia and the other regional leads, then i do one to one with each rep across monday and tuesday, midweek is usually customer escalations or joining calls when a rep asks for help on a bigger deal, friday is the pipeline review with the whole team where we go deal by deal and stress test what's in commit and best case. fridays often run long.

## Phase 2: Knowledge & Expertise

### Q: How did sea land in q1?

**A:** we came in within six percent of plan which i'm honestly proud of given the migration noise and the funnel shift. the number was nine point four against ten. enterprise carried us, marcus had two big closes in march that pulled us out of the hole. smb was softer than i wanted but not catastrophic.

### Q: What's the hardest kind of deal for you to forecast right now?

**A:** the ones where the merchant says we need to think about it. those kill me. the rep marks it best case, sometimes commit, and then it sits for six weeks and you find out they went with someone else or just did nothing. the false positive rate on we need to think is very high. i've started telling reps unless there's a written next step from the merchant treat it as pipeline not commit, but it's a habit thing.

### Q: How do you think about the 80 calls a day target for outbound?

**A:** ok so this is a thing. i enforce it because that's what we agreed and the floor needs the discipline. but i don't think it's the right metric anymore. the funnel has shifted, the leads coming in are warmer on the inbound side and colder on the outbound list and eighty dials a day is a number from when the lists were better. i've flagged it to sofia twice. she agrees in principle but we haven't changed it. so until we change it it's eighty.

## Phase 3: Processes & Tools

### Q: How is the hubspot migration going on your side?

**A:** mostly done. the pipeline migrated, the deal stages are mapped, the dashboards work for what i need. there are weird edges — some contact records lost their notes during the migration which the reps grumble about — but eighty percent of what we do day to day works. the bigger issue was training. it was rushed. we got two sessions and a recording and then we were live. some of the reps still don't know where to find the deal history filter and they ask me. that should have been a week of proper enablement not two sessions.

### Q: Anything else on tooling that bugs you?

**A:** lead routing from marketing. it's inconsistent. sometimes a lead comes in and is assigned within twenty minutes which is fine, sometimes it sits unassigned for four hours before someone notices. four hours on a hot inbound is a lost lead in this market. i don't know if that's a hubspot config thing or a marketing process thing but rajesh's team and mine need to sort it out.
