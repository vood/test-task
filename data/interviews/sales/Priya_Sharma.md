## Phase 1: Role & Daily Work

### Q: Priya — and yes i know, the third priya — give me the short version.

**A:** ha. yeah, third priya. running joke. priya raman is the coo, priya devi is in people, i'm the inbound sales one. when someone slacks "hey priya" in a public channel all three of us reply. anyway — i handle inbound demo requests in sea. about thirty demos a week. anything that comes through the website or a content marketing form and clears the qualification bar gets routed to me. i report to aisha yusof.

### Q: What's your daily pattern.

**A:** mornings i prep the day's demos. usually six to eight a day, thirty to forty five minutes each. i pull whatever i can find on the merchant ahead of time — website, what they sell, rough size. afternoons are demos and follow-ups. fridays i try to push through any deals close to closing.

## Phase 2: Knowledge & Expertise

### Q: How long have you been here?

**A:** six months. joined in october.

### Q: What do you sell mostly?

**A:** smb, almost entirely. helixpay core plus pos for the merchants that have a physical storefront. occasionally loyalty as an add-on but loyalty is more of a marcus motion when it's enterprise. typical deal i close is eight hundred to fifteen hundred sgd mrr. i close maybe three or four a month. small but volume.

### Q: How does lead routing work.

**A:** honestly i'm a bit confused by it. it's hubspot rules and depending on the signals — company size, geography, what they filled in — leads go to me or to kenji or to marcus. i've had cases where a lead i thought was clearly mine went to kenji and cases where i got a lead that probably should have gone to marcus because the company turned out to be bigger than the form said. i've asked aisha about it once and she explained part of it but i couldn't tell you the full logic. i mostly trust the routing and chase down anything that feels off.

## Phase 3: Processes & Tools

### Q: How's hubspot for you.

**A:** mostly fine actually. i joined after the migration so i never used pipedrive here, hubspot is just my crm. i hear marcus and kenji complain about history not migrating cleanly but i don't have that pain because i don't have history from before. the things that annoy me are small — the activity logging is clunky, sequences sometimes don't trigger when i think they should. nothing dealbreaking.

### Q: Anything else in your stack.

**A:** zoom for demos, gong for call recording, slack, google calendar, a shared notion page where the team puts demo notes and competitive intel.

## Phase 5: Problems & Ideas

### Q: If you could fix one thing about your day what would it be.

**A:** demo prep. right now i spend maybe twenty minutes per demo digging through the merchant's website and sometimes their socials trying to figure out what they actually do and what they'd care about. half the time i'm still guessing when the call starts. if there were a way to just ask "give me a brief on this merchant before my eleven am demo" and get something useful, that's an hour of my day back.

### Q: What about routing — would you change that?

**A:** maybe. i'd at least like to understand it better. when a lead skips me i'd like to know why. right now it's a black box.
