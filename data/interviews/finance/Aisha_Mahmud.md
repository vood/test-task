## Phase 2: Knowledge & Expertise

### Q: Give me the q1 numbers.

**A:** q1 revenue came in at sgd fourteen point two million against a plan of sixteen million. that's about eleven percent under plan. ebitda loss of two point one million which is an improvement from minus three point four in q1 last year. monthly burn one point eight million sgd. cash runway eighteen months at current burn. that's the headline.

### Q: Sea versus brasil split?

**A:** sea revenue around nine point four million against a target of ten. brasil four point eight million sgd which is twenty-two million reais against a target of twenty-eight million reais — equivalent to roughly six million sgd at plan fx. so brasil missed harder than sea both in local currency and in sgd terms. fx didn't help — the real weakened against the sgd over the quarter so the sgd-equivalent miss is bigger than the local miss. that's a recurring theme.

### Q: Net new merchants?

**A:** four hundred twelve net new merchants in q1 against plan of three hundred eighty. so we beat on logo count even though we missed on revenue. that tells you the deals coming in are smaller than planned which lines up with what fernanda's been seeing on the marketing side and what maria silva's team is reporting on average deal size. total paid merchants now around three thousand four hundred.

### Q: What's driving the brasil miss?

**A:** mix of things. lower average deal size from the q4 marketing expansion bringing in smaller merchants. the tap launch generated leads but conversion to paid was weaker than we modeled. the q1 enterprise churns hit some of the brazil arr — cosmos hotels alone was a meaningful chunk of the three hundred forty thousand combined arr we lost. and fx as i mentioned. the underlying business is growing but slower than plan and the fx compounds it on the consolidated number.

### Q: How painful is the sea-brasil consolidation work?

**A:** painful. we run two general ledgers — one in singapore on netsuite and one in brasil on a local erp — and consolidate monthly. the consolidation involves fx translation, intercompany eliminations, and a chunk of manual reclassifications because the chart of accounts isn't fully harmonized yet. carlos's team in são paulo and my team in singapore basically spend the first week of every month on this. it eats time we should spend on analysis. we have a project to harmonize the coa but it's been deprioritized twice. the fx assumptions matter a lot for the consolidated brasil number — a five percent move in usd-brl flows through to the headline.

### Q: Q2 target — is it being revised?

**A:** that conversation is in flight. boon hock and i are working through what a credible q2 plan looks like given the q1 miss and the underlying trends. we've shared a draft revision with wei and priya. i don't want to pre-empt the board conversation but the direction is to revise q2 and full year down to something we can actually hit. the runway math at one point eight million burn gives us eighteen months which is comfortable but not infinite — we don't want to optimize for a plan number we can't hit.

### Q: Board pack — when does it land?

**A:** board meeting is may 12. the pack goes out a week before per the cadence. so i need a final draft to boon hock by end of next week. q1 actuals are basically done, the q2 outlook is the work in front of us. there's a re-baseline happening on confluence around april thirty per daniel's plan and the board needs to be briefed on that too — that's separate work but the timing matters because it affects the technology spend assumptions in the model.

## Phase 5: Problems & Ideas

### Q: If you had a system with perfect context what would change?

**A:** the close cycle would compress. right now first week of the month is mostly mechanical — pulling data from netsuite, the brasil erp, salesforce, looker, reconciling intercompany, getting fx right, building the management report. if an agent could pull the data, do the obvious reconciliations, flag anomalies, and present me a draft management report on day three i'd get four days back every month. that's a quarter of my team's capacity. and on the strategic side — board pack prep is a lot of stitching together different decks. if the underlying numbers were always live and consistent the narrative work would be the only manual part.

### Q: Anything on the cross-functional side?

**A:** the gap between what sales reports as pipeline and what finance recognizes as revenue. there's always a translation. an agent that bridged sales and finance views with consistent definitions would save a lot of arguments.

### Q: Gap between leadership story and reality?

**A:** the public number is "we're growing" and that's true. the more nuanced story is we're growing slower than plan, the brasil business is harder than expected, and the runway clock is running. the board will get the nuanced version on may 12. the all-hands will get the optimistic version. that's not unusual for a series b company but it's worth noting.
