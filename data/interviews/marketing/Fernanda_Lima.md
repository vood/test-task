## Phase 2: Knowledge & Expertise

### Q: Give me the headline on brasil marketing performance.

**A:** lead volume up forty percent year over year which sounds great. but lead quality is down — conversion to paid is materially weaker than this time last year. so we're filling more top of funnel and the bottom of funnel isn't keeping up. the net effect on revenue is okay but not what the lead number suggests.

### Q: What changed?

**A:** in q4 we did an expansion push — broader targeting, broader keywords, more content for non-core verticals. the assumption was we'd find new merchant pockets that the narrow targeting was missing. we did find some but we also brought in a lot of leads that aren't really our icp. the targeting got too broad. eu acho que a gente exagerou. i pushed back on the breadth at the time but the q4 number was the priority and broader targeting moves the lead count fast.

### Q: How does that show up?

**A:** sdr conversations are harder because the leads aren't pre-qualified well. bruno and ana beatriz tell me they spend more time disqualifying than they used to. demo-to-paid is down. and the leads we are converting are smaller — average deal size is down maybe fifteen percent versus q4. so we're acquiring smaller merchants in larger numbers which is not the strategy we said we were running.

### Q: Tap launch — how did marketing go?

**A:** we launched tap in brasil in q4 2025. it generated a lot of leads, much more than core launches typically do, because tap-on-phone is genuinely interesting to a segment of merchants who couldn't justify a card reader. the lead volume was great. the conversion to paid is weak. part of that is the targeting issue, part is that some of the merchants who came in for tap are very small and have low transaction volume so even when they convert they don't move the revenue line much. we're recalibrating.

### Q: What does the landing page testing tell you?

**A:** this is the interesting one. we've been a/b testing messaging on the brasil landing pages. discount messaging — "save fifteen percent on processing" — converts about three times better than feature messaging — "fast settlement, brazilian compliance built in". três vezes melhor. that tells me the brasil market right now is pricing-sensitive in a way that isn't reflected in our positioning. sea data shows the opposite — feature messaging wins there. so we're running essentially two different positioning strategies in the two geos which is fine but it's a real signal about what merchants here actually care about.

### Q: Have you raised the pricing pressure signal?

**A:** yes, with rajesh and i mentioned it to sofia almeida last month. it's known. whether anything will change with pricing is a separate conversation that's above me. but the data is clear and i wanted it on the record.

### Q: How does the singapore-brasil marketing relationship work?

## Phase 5: Problems & Ideas

### Q: If a system gave your team perfect context what changes?

**A:** two things. one — i'd want a feedback loop from sales to marketing on lead quality that's faster than the current monthly review. if maria and joão's team flagged in real time which leads are worth talking to and which aren't we could adjust paid faster. two — i'd want a content agent that could keep our portuguese content in sync with the global content. right now wendy ships something in english and it takes us three weeks to localize and sometimes the local version drifts from the global brand voice.

### Q: Gap between leadership story and your reality?

**A:** the lead volume number gets celebrated and the lead quality story gets a footnote. that's the gap. forty percent up year on year is the line in the all-hands deck and the conversion-to-paid number doesn't get the same airtime.
