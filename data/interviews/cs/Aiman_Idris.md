## Phase 1: Role & Daily Work

### Q: Aiman, what do you own?

**A:** about twenty-five mid-market accounts in sea, mostly singapore and malaysia, a couple in thailand. i report to tom holloway, tom reports to marco. been here just over two years. senior csm so i also informally mentor the two newer csms on the team but it's not a formal manager thing.

### Q: What does a typical day look like?

**A:** customer calls in the morning usually, two or three of them, then internal stuff in the afternoon, ticket triage, prep, slack with engineering when something's stuck. i spend more time than i should chasing internal answers for merchants honestly.

## Phase 2: Knowledge & Expertise

### Q: Tell me about a recent save.

**A:** sg retail chain, eight stores, mid-market account, was about to leave us over the tap-loyalty bug. their reconciliation reports were showing mismatches between tap transactions and loyalty point accrual which meant their finance team couldn't close the books cleanly at month-end. for a retail merchant that's a hard no, you can't tell them "wait three months for a fix" when they have to file. i pulled in ahmad rashid's team — they own the mobile side which is where the bug actually is — and we hashed out a workaround. ahmad's team generates a manual reconciliation report for them once a month, separately, that ties out the numbers. we send it to the merchant's finance lead. it's not a real fix but it bought us the relationship until the actual fix lands.

### Q: When does the actual fix land?

**A:** i don't actually know. that's part of the problem.

### Q: Say more about that.

**A:** so i can't see engineering's ticket queue. ahmad told me "soon" in march, then "we're trying for end of q2," and i don't have a way to verify that or tell the merchant something more specific. when the merchant asks me — and they do ask, every month when i send them the manual report — i either repeat what ahmad said last or i ping ahmad again. it's not a great look. if i could see the ticket status myself i'd at least be able to say "still in progress, last updated last week" without bothering ahmad.

## Phase 3: Processes & Tools

### Q: Walk me through your qbr prep.

**A:** so for each account i open — let me actually count — gainsight for csm history, zendesk for tickets, the product warehouse for usage data, hubspot or pipedrive depending on whether the account has migrated, intercom for chat history, and slack search for any threads about the merchant. that's six tabs. sometimes seven if i need finance. and i'm copy-pasting between them into a doc.

### Q: How long?

**A:** two and a half to three hours per qbr. tom said the same, it tracks. the actual qbr meeting is forty-five minutes. so for a top-twenty account we spend three hours of csm time to prepare for a forty-five minute meeting. that ratio is wrong.

### Q: Has anyone tried to fix it?

**A:** there was a thing last year where someone tried to build a unified dashboard in retool that pulled from gainsight and zendesk. it half-worked, then the schema changed in zendesk and nobody owned the maintenance, then it died. now we just go back to the tabs.

## Phase 5: Problems & Ideas

### Q: What's the thing you'd want most?

**A:** two things actually. one, the qbr brief that tom is asking for, give me a single document that pulls the six tabs together, that's the obvious one. two — and this is more specific to the senior csm thing — visibility into engineering. let me see, for a given product issue, what ticket is tracking it, what the status is, when it was last touched, what the eta is even if soft. right now i'm a black box to my merchants on engineering questions and that's the most uncomfortable part of the job.

### Q: Anything else?

**A:** product feedback loop. i tell beatriz oliveira's team about a brasil-side issue or hannah park's team about an sea issue, i don't know what happens after. did it become a ticket? is it on the backlog? is it being prioritized? did anyone else flag the same thing? marco talks about wanting to close that loop and i think he means it but it hasn't happened yet.

## Phase 6: Calibration

### Q: Anything else worth knowing about how cs works on the ground?

**A:** the workarounds we run are real work. the manual reconciliation report for that retail chain — ahmad's eng on it once a month, my time on it once a month, plus the merchant's finance team time. that's hidden cost we're paying because the bug isn't fixed. multiply that by however many merchants are affected by the same bug and it's a lot of swallowed labor that doesn't show up anywhere on a dashboard.
