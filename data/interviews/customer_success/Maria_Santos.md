## Phase 2: Knowledge & Expertise

### Q: Tell me about the at-risk book.

**A:** fourteen accounts under save-plan, all tier-two smb basically, combined arr around one point one million reais. eight of them have active save-plan motions running, csm calls weekly or biweekly, sometimes a discount or a service credit on the table, sometimes a feature request being escalated. the other six are in monitoring because we don't have the people to run intervention on all fourteen. tá complicado. if i had my full headcount we'd be intervening on all of them.

### Q: What's driving the risk?

**A:** two things mainly. one is response time on support — we're missing target on all three tiers in brasil right now, i think marco told you the numbers, three point five hours on tier one against a two hour target, eleven hours on tier two against eight, thirty hours on tier three against twenty-four. when small merchants don't get answers in the time they expect, they start shopping. two is the tap-loyalty integration bug. for tier-two smb specifically that's a brutal one because they're small finance teams, often the owner is doing their own books, and when their reconciliation reports don't tie out at month-end they can't just absorb that the way a big merchant could.

### Q: How bad is the tap-loyalty thing in brasil?

**A:** it's the single most cited complaint in our q1 brasil smb survey verbatims. i don't have an exact number in front of me but it's mentioned in i'd say at least a third of the negative responses. i've been escalating it to beatriz oliveira — she's the product lead in brasil, she's been pushing it up to hannah park's team in singapore. last update i had was that it's on the backlog and being worked but no firm date. meanwhile we're losing tier-twos over it. a gente tá perdendo gente por causa disso.

### Q: And the brasil smb nps?

**A:** thirty-one. last quarter it was thirty-nine, so down eight points. that's the worst movement of any segment we measure. four hundred and eight respondents so it's not a sample size issue, the number is real. some of that is the support response time, some is the tap-loyalty bug, some is just that we lost two csms and the relationships got thinner during february and march. all three things compound on each other.

### Q: The two csm losses — what happened?

**A:** one went to a competitor, totalpay, they made her a senior csm offer with a meaningful raise and i couldn't match within band. that was end of january. the other one is on maternity leave starting mid-february, she'll be back but not until later in the year. the maternity one we knew was coming and i had a backfill plan but then the totalpay one happened on top and we just didn't have the bench. roberta nunes in hr has been helpful on the hiring but the brasil cs market is small and the candidates that are good are getting pulled in multiple directions.

### Q: Any of the at-risk accounts you can share specifics on?

**A:** i don't want to name them on a transcript but the pattern is consistent — small to mid-sized retail or f&b, on tap and loyalty, hit the reconciliation issue, called support, didn't get a fast answer, csm coverage was thin, and now they're talking to competitors. that's the modal story. eight to ten of the fourteen fit that exact shape.

## Phase 5: Problems & Ideas

### Q: If you could fix one thing for the brasil cs team in the next ninety days, what?

**A:** support response time. that's the foundation. without that the save-plans don't work because the customer is already frustrated by the time the csm gets involved. dewi lestari runs sea support, brasil support reports up through marco a different way, but we need more capacity in brasil and faster routing. the rest — qbr prep, ai briefs, all of that — is real but if i had to pick one thing, it's support response time.

### Q: And the second thing?

**A:** the qbr prep automation that tom is asking for, yes, we want it too. give me a customer brief for any merchant in five minutes instead of three hours. especially with the team short, every hour we save on prep is an hour we can put on save-plan calls.

### Q: What about the tap-loyalty bug?

**A:** fix it. that's not a process problem, that's an engineering problem. beatriz has been escalating, hannah's team has it on the backlog, daniel tan's confluence work is consuming a lot of engineering capacity right now. i understand the constraint but that bug is costing us merchants in brasil today.

### Q: Anything you wish leadership understood better about brasil cs?

**A:** that the brasil business is not a smaller version of the sea business. the merchant profile is different, the support expectations are different, the competitive landscape is different — totalpay and a couple of others are aggressive on price and on csm relationships. when sea benchmarks are applied to brasil — like "look, sea response time is fine" — that doesn't translate. rafael gets it because he's run brasil for years. marco gets it because he came in through the brasil acquisition. but at the singapore-hq level i'm not sure it always lands. olha só, the nps gap between sea enterprise sixty-two and brasil smb thirty-one tells the story if anyone wants to read it.
