## Phase 2: Knowledge & Expertise

### Q: The sea enterprise nps is sixty-two. talk to me about that number.

**A:** yeah it's a number i'm proud of. that segment was in the low fifties when i took over the role and we've moved it up over the last year and change. forty-seven respondents which is a decent sample for that segment size. but i want to be really clear — that's sea enterprise. the aggregate across the company is forty-seven, brazil smb is thirty-one, those are different stories. when people internally cite "our nps is sixty-two" that's not really what's going on. marco and i are aligned on that, we both want the aggregate to be the lead number externally.

### Q: Walk me through the cosmos hotels loss.

**A:** that one stings. i was the csm on cosmos for about fourteen months. marcus lee was the ae on the original deal and stayed involved on the relationship side. they're a regional hotel chain, multi-property, and from pretty early on they were asking for consolidated reporting across their properties — they wanted to see one dashboard with all their locations rolling up, settlement comparisons across properties, the full thing. our product treats each property as its own merchant entity essentially so the reporting was per-property. i escalated the gap to product about eleven months ago, opened a ticket, talked to hannah's team, it went on the backlog. it didn't move. they renewed once with us hoping it would come and then this past quarter they signed with a competitor that has the multi-property view native. lost on product not on price. that's the cosmos story.

### Q: Did you see it coming?

**A:** yeah. their qbr in q4 was tense. their cfo basically said "we're going to give you one more cycle." i flagged it red on the renewals tracker and marco knew. but knowing isn't the same as having the feature.

### Q: And lazada singapore?

**A:** different story. lazada sg midmarket, that was pricing. their contract came up in february and a local competitor came in with a number we couldn't match for that volume tier. we tried, sofia approved a discount, it still wasn't enough. we lost it in q1. that one was less of a surprise, more of a "this is what the market looks like at that segment right now."

### Q: How do you decide a customer is at-risk before it gets bad?

**A:** three signals i look at, in this order. login frequency — if a merchant's daily active sessions on the dashboard drop noticeably for two weeks running, something's off. ticket volume — but you have to read it carefully, sometimes more tickets means more usage, sometimes it means more pain, you have to actually look at the ticket content. and nps and qbr verbatims, the qualitative stuff. those three together usually give me a feel. it's not a model, it's a habit.

## Phase 3: Processes & Tools

### Q: Tell me about the qbr process.

**A:** we run quarterly business reviews with our top twenty sea accounts. that's the cadence — top twenty by arr, refreshed each quarter. for each one i or one of my csms does a full prep, builds a deck, runs the meeting, writes up actions and circulates. the meeting itself is fine, the prep is the killer.

### Q: How long does prep take?

**A:** about three hours per account on average. some are faster if it's a clean account with steady metrics, some are way longer if there's a save-plan or product issues going on. and most of those three hours is not thinking — it's stitching. i pull usage data from the product warehouse, ticket history from zendesk, csm notes from gainsight, deal context and renewal date from pipedrive — well, hubspot now, for the accounts that migrated, pipedrive for the ones that didn't yet. and then nps verbatims if they've responded to a survey recently. five tools to build one brief.

### Q: Anything else in that flow?

**A:** finance — i sometimes need to check invoice payment status because late payment is a churn signal. that's another tool. and slack search if i remember a conversation about the customer happened. so call it six.

### Q: How do your csms feel about it?

**A:** aiman complains about it openly which is fair. he's senior so he can say it. the more junior csms just grind through it. it's a tax and we all know it but nobody has time to fix it.

## Phase 5: Problems & Ideas

### Q: If you could wave a wand and have one thing built for the cs team, what is it?

**A:** ai prep for qbrs. give me a customer brief. type in the merchant name, it pulls everything from the last six months — usage trends, ticket history with sentiment, csm notes, renewal date, payment behavior, nps responses, recent product issues affecting them — into one document i can actually read in fifteen minutes instead of three hours. and ideally it surfaces the things that matter, not just the data. like "this account had two sev-2 tickets in march, login activity dropped twenty percent the week after, last csm note flagged frustration with the loyalty reconciliation." that's the difference between data and a brief. that's what i want.

### Q: What about ongoing, not just qbr prep?

**A:** same thing but pushed to me. if the system sees the pattern i described it should ping me, not wait for me to ask. but qbr prep is the wedge because it's the thing that's eating time today.

### Q: What do you not want it to do?

**A:** i don't want it to write the qbr deck for me. i want it to inform the deck. the customer relationship is the relationship and i don't want a generated narrative going to a customer with my name on it. brief me, don't speak for me.

### Q: Anything else broken in the cs stack?

**A:** the gainsight to hubspot bridge is messy right now because the migration is in flight. some sea accounts are already in hubspot, some still in pipedrive, and gainsight reads from the salesforce-to-gainsight connector which doesn't fully understand the new state. for about another month or two we have inconsistent renewal date data across systems. that's an annoyance.
