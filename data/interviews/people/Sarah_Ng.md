## Phase 2: Knowledge & Expertise

### Q: Q1 attrition numbers — what are they.

**A:** annualized, sea is at six point eight percent, brasil at eleven point two. the brasil number is higher because of the two csm departures plus an engineer leaving in march. small numbers in absolute terms — we're a two hundred seventy five person company so a couple of people moves the rate a lot — but the brasil cs attrition is the one to watch because it's pattern not noise. roberta has flagged it. those two csms both went to local fintechs offering more.

### Q: Where's hiring right now?

**A:** sea engineering is on hold pending the confluence re-baseline. daniel and arjun made that call last week and i agree with it — we should not be onboarding new engineers into a program that is about to be re-scoped. we'll un-pause once the re-baseline is signed off, which i'm told is end of april. brasil engineering hiring is continuing, three open reqs there. sales hiring is continuing in both geos. i have an open vp finance partner search that's been slow and an enterprise ae search in sea that's also been slow.

### Q: What's the topic you get the most pushback on from managers?

**A:** onboarding. by a wide margin. managers tell me — and they've been telling me for a year — that new joiners take four to six weeks to ramp, sometimes longer for engineers, more like two to three months for someone joining the platform team. the pushback i get is "your onboarding program isn't doing the job." and i hear it but the actual problem is that the knowledge is in five engineers' heads, not in any document, and no amount of pre-canned onboarding curriculum fixes that. i can run a great two-week structured program and someone is still going to spend their next eight weeks dm'ing sara wijaya to figure out how anything actually works.

## Phase 5: Problems & Ideas

### Q: If the codos engagement delivered exactly what it promises, what's your ask.

**A:** cut onboarding time. genuinely. if a new joiner can ask "how does the brazil tap-loyalty integration handle a refund" and get a real answer with the relevant context instead of pinging an engineer, that is the single biggest people-ops unlock i can think of. i've talked about this with priya raman — for me this is the headline outcome of the engagement. i'm less invested in the sales agent or the exec briefing piece. onboarding is where the pain is.

### Q: What's the gap between what leadership thinks people-ops is doing and what's actually happening.

**A:** there's a perception that we under-invest in onboarding and that's why ramp is slow. the reality is we invest a normal amount and ramp is slow because the company doesn't write things down. that distinction matters because the fix isn't more onboarding budget, it's tooling and culture around documentation. the codos work is exactly that fix in my read.
