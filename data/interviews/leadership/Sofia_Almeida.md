## Phase 2: Knowledge & Expertise

### Q: Where did q1 land?

**A:** fourteen point two million sgd against sixteen plan. eleven percent miss. sea was within six percent which is fine, brazil was twenty percent under. the underlying issue in brazil isn't volume — we signed more merchants than we planned to — it's deal size. average revenue per new merchant in brazil dropped about twenty-five percent year on year. there are three reasons. one is segment shift, we're going beyond são paulo into smaller markets where merchant size is smaller. two is product mix, the tap product is bringing in smaller initial deals and the loyalty attach hasn't followed. three is competitive pressure on price in the smb segment.

### Q: Q2 looks tough. What's the plan?

**A:** sea i'm holding to plan, the pipeline is there. brazil i'm cleaning up the pipeline before i commit. probably going to wei with a revised q2 number rather than holding the original and missing. but i'm holding the conversation until early may.

### Q: The crm migration — what's the actual state?

**A:** we are at seventy-eight percent of pipeline value in hubspot. that's a sea-weighted number though — the brazil team has not started. we said we'd start the brazil migration on may first and finish by end of june. that timeline is tight. realistically i think it's mid-july. i committed to end of june in the weekly review.

### Q: Why hasn't brazil started yet?

**A:** because the brazil pipeline data is messier than the sea pipeline data. there are duplicate accounts because the pre-acquisition company didn't have account dedup discipline and we're going to clean it up on the way into hubspot. that takes a couple of weeks of revops time which we couldn't free up in q1 because everyone was on the migration of the sea side.

### Q: Anything else painful in sales right now?

**A:** the eighty calls per day target for outbound. we set it a year ago when the funnel was different, the funnel is now different, and the team is still being measured against it. so the team is making the calls but a lot of those calls are low quality follow-ups to recycled leads. we should have changed the metric six months ago. that's on me.

## Phase 3: Processes & Tools

### Q: Tools?

**A:** hubspot in sea, pipedrive in brazil. salesloft for cadences in sea. apollo for prospecting. gong for call recordings, sea only. brazil records calls but doesn't have the analysis layer.

### Q: Lead flow?

**A:** marketing-generated leads come through paid ads, organic content, and partner referrals. they hit hubspot in sea or pipedrive in brazil. round-robin assignment to outbound reps. inbound team handles inbound demo requests separately. sdr team handles outbound prospecting. enterprise deals route directly to marcus lee in sea or larissa mendes / maria silva in brazil. handoff to onboarding when payment is received.

## Phase 5: Problems & Ideas

### Q: What would change if the company had an internal agent that could answer questions about pipeline and customers reliably?

**A:** sales prep is the obvious one. for an enterprise deal i want a brief that pulls every interaction we've ever had with that company across slack email zendesk gong and the crm. if i could ask "give me the last six months on cosmos hotels" and get a real answer with sources, my prep time goes to a third of what it is now. that's table stakes. the more valuable thing is honest pipeline review. right now i ask sales people what's likely to close and they tell me what they want me to hear. if a system could ground the conversation in actual signal — last touch date, response cadence, internal stakeholders engaged — pipeline accuracy goes up.

### Q: What's the gap between the company's story about sales and the reality?

**A:** the brazil number. and the eighty-call target. if you read internal communication you'd think we're a couple of months from fixing brazil and that the team is hitting activity targets. neither is fully true.
