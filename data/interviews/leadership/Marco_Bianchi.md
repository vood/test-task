## Phase 1: Role & Daily Work

### Q: Marco, give me your function in one breath.

**A:** customer success and support for the whole company across both geos. that's tom holloway running csm in singapore, maria santos running csm in brazil, dewi lestari running support in sea, and the support contractors out of manila. about thirty people total counting the contractors. i report to priya raman.

### Q: Where do you spend most of your time?

**A:** about forty percent on customer escalations directly because we don't have a layer between me and the bigger customers yet, thirty percent on the brazil side because that's where the heat is, twenty on hiring and the team, ten on cross-functional stuff like the ai engagement and product feedback loops. the forty percent number is bad and i'm trying to fix it.

## Phase 2: Knowledge & Expertise

### Q: Talk to me about NPS. The number people throw around is 62. What's the real story?

**A:** sixty-two is the sea enterprise number. it's a real number and i'm proud of it — that segment was at fifty-three a year ago. but the aggregate number across all four segments and both geos is forty-seven and the brazil smb number is thirty-one which is bad. i've been asking for a year to lead with the aggregate when we communicate externally because the segmented view is what we use internally and the aggregate is what reflects the company. sofia wants to keep the segmented view because the enterprise number is the fundraising story. wei hasn't decided. i think we'll converge but it's a live debate.

### Q: Why is brazil smb at thirty-one?

**A:** two reasons mostly. one is that we lost two csms in q1 in brazil — one to a competitor and one to maternity leave — and we didn't backfill fast enough. that meant our case response time roughly doubled in february and march. with a customer base that's mostly small merchants where the csm relationship is the relationship, that hurts a lot fast. two is the tap product launched there and the tap-loyalty integration has had bugs that are visible to the merchant in their reconciliation reports. small merchants notice when their reports are wrong.

### Q: What's the at-risk situation in brazil?

**A:** maria santos's team is tracking fourteen tier-two accounts with deteriorating engagement, that's accounts where login frequency, support cases, or invoice payment behavior has shifted in a bad direction. combined arr is about one point one million reais. she has save plans in motion on eight of them. the other six i think we lose unless we can fix the support response time, which is the more fundamental issue.

### Q: What did the q1 enterprise churns teach you?

**A:** the three churns were lazada singapore midmarket, cosmos hotels, and banco verde. lazada was pricing, the contract came up for renewal and our pricing wasn't competitive against a cheaper local competitor for the volume tier they were in. cosmos hotels we lost to a competitor — not on price, on product, they wanted multi-property reporting that we don't have yet. banco verde was a internal bank decision, they're consolidating payment vendors as part of their own restructuring, that one is not really about us. the lesson from cosmos is real — multi-property is a gap and we know it.

### Q: What does support response time look like?

**A:** target is two hours for tier one, eight hours for tier two, twenty-four for tier three. sea is at one point eight, six point three, twenty-one. we're hitting target. brazil is at three point five, eleven, thirty. we're missing on all three tiers. that's the headline.

## Phase 3: Processes & Tools

### Q: What's your tool stack?

**A:** zendesk for tickets, gainsight for csm, slack for internal, intercom for chat on the merchant portal. and a custom internal tool that the brazil team built before the acquisition that maria santos's team still uses for some workflows because gainsight wasn't translated to portuguese until last year and the team got attached to their thing. consolidating that is on the q3 list.

### Q: Where do you store customer health data?

**A:** gainsight is the system of record. we sync usage data from the product warehouse via a nightly job, finance data via the salesforce-to-gainsight connector, and case data via the zendesk integration. it works reasonably well. the gap is qualitative signal — the things csms hear in calls don't always make it back into the system.

## Phase 5: Problems & Ideas

### Q: If you had a system that could read every customer interaction and give you the truth on health, what would change?

**A:** the easy answer is i'd catch at-risk earlier. the more interesting answer is i'd have a way to close the loop on product feedback. right now if maria santos's team hears "we hate the reconciliation report" from twelve customers, that becomes a slack message to beatriz, beatriz adds it to a backlog, eventually it gets prioritized or doesn't, and the csm never knows whether they were heard. if that pattern were aggregated automatically and shown to product and to me with a proper count and the verbatims, the prioritization conversation would be different.

### Q: What's the gap between the leadership story and the cs reality right now?

**A:** the nps gap. the brazil tier two churn risk. and the support response time in brazil. i don't think any of those are hidden — wei and priya know — but the all-hands tone last week was more upbeat than the weekly review tone. that's not unusual but candidates for the codos work should know that what people hear in all-hands is not the full picture.
