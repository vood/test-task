## Phase 1: Role & Daily Work

### Q: Vinod — what do you own in one sentence?

**A:** i own pos self-service end to end. the kiosk product. that means roadmap, prioritization with eng, partnership with the hardware vendor, go-to-market with rajesh's team, and field feedback from the merchants who actually have the kiosks. i report to hannah.

### Q: How does a typical week look?

**A:** mondays the global product review with hannah and the other pms. tuesday is usually a hardware partner sync and a deployment review with the field ops folks. wednesday and thursday are heads-down weeks ideally — backlog grooming, design reviews, jira hygiene. fridays i try to do customer calls, usually two or three with merchants who already have kiosks. that's where i learn the most.

## Phase 2: Knowledge & Expertise

### Q: Give me the real number on adoption.

**A:** we launched pos self-service in 2025. as of last week we have roughly three hundred forty active kiosks across sea. our plan said six hundred by end of q1 2026 so we are about forty-three percent under plan. that's the headline and i'm not going to dress it up.

### Q: Why are we behind?

**A:** two main reasons. first is hardware. the kiosks have a three-month lead time from our manufacturing partner. when we placed orders in q4 expecting a strong q1 we underestimated the q1 demand from a couple of larger merchants and we ran out of units in february. so even where we had signed deals we couldn't deploy fast enough. second reason is more painful — there is real market confusion between "helixpay pos" and "helixpay pos self-service". they're different products. pos is the card reader plus app, the merchant takes the payment. pos self-service is the kiosk, the customer takes the payment themselves. but the names are basically the same and even our internal sales reps mix them up.

### Q: How often does the internal mixup happen?

**A:** more than i'd like. i had a sales rep last month forward me an opportunity tagged as pos self-service that was actually a pos deal. and the inverse happens too — merchants signing for pos and then asking why their kiosk isn't shipping. when sales is confused the merchant ends up confused.

### Q: What's your fix?

**A:** i want to merge the marketing landing pages and run them as one product family with two skus or modes underneath. it's clearer to the merchant and the sales narrative gets simpler. but that's a hannah and rajesh conversation and we haven't agreed. rajesh wants to keep them separate because pos self-service has a different buyer persona — bigger merchants, more procurement-driven, longer cycle — and his data says the messaging needs to be different. i hear that but i think the cost of the confusion is bigger than the benefit of two pages.

### Q: Where does the conversation stand?

**A:** stuck. i raised it in february, raised it again in march. hannah's open to it but is pulled into other things. rajesh isn't blocking but he isn't agreeing either. we need a thirty minute meeting and a decision. i should just force it.

## Phase 3: Processes & Tools

### Q: What tools are you running?

**A:** productboard for roadmap, linear for tickets with eng, looker for the kiosk telemetry dashboards, slack for everything. plus a shared sheet with field ops that tracks every deployed kiosk by merchant by site by status. that sheet is the actual source of truth for the install base, looker pulls from it.

### Q: Who's your eng counterpart?

**A:** ahmad rashid is the mobile lead and the kiosk firmware sits under him. there's a small team — three engineers — focused on the kiosk app. they're also blocked on confluence for some platform work but most of the kiosk-specific stuff is independent.

## Phase 5: Problems & Ideas

### Q: If you could fix one thing tomorrow what would it be?

**A:** the naming. genuinely. if i could click a button and merge pos and pos self-service into one branded family with two configurations, i think we'd close more deals and have fewer support tickets and the sales team would thank me. the engineering side i can manage, the customer comms i can manage, the brand thing is the actual blocker.

### Q: And on the system-context side, what would you want from a knowledge agent?

**A:** something that can answer "is this a pos deal or a pos self-service deal" by reading the opportunity and the merchant context. half the internal slacks i get could be answered by an agent. and something that can keep the marketing pages and the internal sales decks consistent — when we update one we forget to update the other and that's part of why the confusion persists.
