# HelixPay — Orientation note

*Written by Priya Raman (COO) for the Codos team starting their engagement, April 22, 2026. Light edit pass only.*

This is meant to give you a map before you dive into the rest of the dump. Treat it as my framing, not as ground truth — when other documents disagree with this, please trust the documents.

## Who we are

HelixPay is a B2B payments company. We started in Singapore in 2018 and we've grown in two halves: a Southeast Asia business run out of HQ (Singapore, Malaysia, Indonesia, Thailand) and a Brazil subsidiary, **HelixPay Brasil** (sometimes you'll see "HPB" or "Helix Brasil" — same thing) that we acquired in late 2023. We closed Series B in mid-2024 (Vertex SE Asia led, Saison and Monk's Hill participating) and we have ~18 months of runway at current burn.

Headcount is around 275 people, split roughly two-thirds SEA, one-third Brazil. The two halves are not yet operationally one company. Bringing them together is the largest piece of work in the building right now.

## What we sell

Five products, in roughly order of revenue:

- **HelixPay Core** — merchant payments processing. Cards, cross-border, FX. The original product. Most of the revenue.
- **HelixPay POS** — physical card-reader terminal plus its companion app. SMB-focused.
- **HelixPay Loyalty** — customer-loyalty add-on that sits on top of POS or Core. Smaller, growing.
- **HelixPay POS Self-Service** — kiosk product, launched 2025. Restaurants and retail. People often shorten it to "Self-Serve" or just "POS Self-Service" or even "POS SS." Not the same as POS.
- **HelixPay Tap** — tap-on-phone, launched in Brazil in Q4 2025. Singapore launch slipped to Q3 2026.

## What's going on right now

Three things matter more than everything else combined.

**Project Confluence.** This is our R&D unification effort — merging the Singapore and Brazil engineering orgs into one platform team with a shared backend. Owner is Daniel Tan, VP Eng. Started in January. The original target was a unified platform live by end of June. There's a lot of detail in the engineering interviews and in the weekly reviews about how this is actually going. The honest answer is "behind."

**CRM migration.** We're partway through moving from Pipedrive to HubSpot. Most of the SEA team has switched. Brazil hasn't. History didn't fully migrate. Sofia Almeida (CRO) owns it. Sales reps have feelings about it. You'll see those feelings in the interviews.

**AI transformation.** Codos engagement, kicked off this month. The point of all of this. I own it from our side.

## The numbers, briefly

Q1 closed at SGD 14.2M revenue against a 16M target. SEA was close to plan, Brazil was about 20% short. We're EBITDA-negative but the trend is the right direction. Net new merchants for the quarter were 412. The full report is in `q1-2026-results.pdf`.

## Things you should know that aren't in the dump

- We have a small data team (3 people) but no dedicated knowledge-management function. Context is in heads.
- Slack is our primary written channel. Email is mostly customer-facing and exec-level.
- We do not have a single source of truth for "who works on what." Roles change faster than the org chart.
- We use Notion for product specs, Google Drive for finance and legal, Linear for engineering tickets, and Pipedrive/HubSpot for sales pipeline. Most of those aren't in this dump — what's here is the human-readable layer.
